I'm a physical chemist, and I've recently finished my PhD at Concordia University under the supervision of <a href="https://www.concordia.ca/faculty/heidi-muchall.html">Heidi Muchall</a> and <a href="https://www.concordia.ca/artsci/chemistry/research/dewolf.html">Christine DeWolf</a>. In my graduate work, I used electronic structure (DFT & semiempirical) methods to understand how phenolic surfactant monolayers self-assemble, organize, and interact with peptides; these surfactants are being developed for surface-based biosensing applications. My teaching assistant experience spans a variety of courses and includes curriculum development, and I've gotten to supervise several undergraduate research projects.

I enjoy using computational tools to help solve chemical puzzles that are difficult to work out by experiment alone, and I'm currently looking for a position where I can bring these skills to new projects!
